ⓘ How the e-Office Score is Calculated
TOPSIS · Winsorized · Peer-relative · Dynamically recalculated on filter
Method: TOPSIS  (Technique for Order of Preference by Similarity to Ideal Solution)

The score measures how geometrically close each officer is to the ideal best performer compared to the ideal worst performer across all 6 metrics simultaneously.

Score = D⁻ ÷ (D⁺ + D⁻) × 100
D⁺ = distance from ideal best  |  D⁻ = distance from ideal worst

Score 100 = as close to ideal best as possible on all metrics.
Score 0 = as far from ideal best as possible.

Winsorization: Before scoring, each metric is capped at the 5th–95th percentile within the peer group. This prevents one extreme outlier from distorting the reference points for everyone else.
▲ Peer-Relative & Dynamic: The ideal best and ideal worst reference points are computed from the currently filtered group of officers. Every time you apply a filter (Cadre, Domicile, Post Type, etc.), the scores recalculate within that subgroup. An officer's score reflects their standing among their peers — not against a fixed absolute target.

Breakdown tooltip: Hover over any score to see each officer's percentile rank on all 6 individual metrics — useful for identifying specific strengths and improvement areas.
