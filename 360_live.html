How do we give the GoI Funds Score? 🏦
A report card for spending money wisely!
The Central Government gives money 💵 to run helpful schemes (roads, schools, toilets…). This score is a report card 🏆 of how fully and wisely each officer used that money. For Secretariat / HoD posts we measure 4 efficiencies, blend them by importance, then make the comparison fair by tenure and fund size — a final score out of 100. 🎯 📄 Read the full methodology →
🛒
40% — BIGGEST · AUE
Spending the Allocation
Expenditure ÷ GoI Allocation. Of all the money sanctioned by the Centre, how much actually reached the ground as spending? The single most important test.
🏗️
30% — FUE
Using Money at the Field
Expenditure ÷ Funds with Agencies. Of the money already sitting with implementing agencies, how much did they actually put to work?
📥
20% — FTE
Pulling the Money In
Funds Transferred ÷ Allocation. Of the money promised, how much did they draw down for implementation? Don't leave it at the Centre.
🏦
10% — UFS · LESS IDLE = MORE
Nothing Left Untapped
100 − (Untapped ÷ Transferred). Money drawn but left lying idle scores low. The smaller the untapped pile, the higher the marks.
🎯 Efficiency = 0.40·AUE + 0.30·FUE + 0.20·FTE + 0.10·UFS (already 0–100). Then two fairness adjustments: ⏱️ each post counts in proportion to how long the officer held it (tenure-weighted), and ⚖️ the score is scaled by a fund-size (difficulty) factor so that spending a ₹5 Cr fund well never beats spending a ₹5,000 Cr fund well — because doing it with bigger money is harder. Final = Efficiency × Difficulty factor. Click Schemes to see the money behind each score!
📍 Different posts, different data:
• Secretariat / HoD posts (Secretariat → by Department, HoD → by HoD, Chief Secretary → everything) have the full 5 figures (Allocation, Transferred, With Agencies, Expenditure, Untapped) → all 4 indicators apply.
• District posts have only Funds Available and Expenditure, so they use a single District Utilization Efficiency = Expenditure ÷ Funds Available, summed across every scheme in that district. It is tenure-weighted and difficulty-scaled the same way, and blended with any head-office posts the officer held.

How do we give the KPI Score? ⭐
Collecting gold stars for great work!
Think of KPIs like gold stars ⭐ that a school gives for doing a great job! Some stars are for a whole district 🌾, some for a department 🏛️, and some for a big boss (HoD) 👑. Every officer collects the right stars based on the job they were doing.
🌾
DISTRICT JOB
Working in a District
If the officer looks after a district, they collect that district's gold stars.
🏛️
SECRETARIAT JOB
Running a Department
If they run a department from the head office, they get the department's average stars.
👑
HoD JOB
Head of a Wing
If they're the head (HoD) of a wing, they collect that wing's average stars.
⏳ The longer an officer stayed in a job, the more those stars count. We then mix all their jobs together (longer jobs count more) to make one shiny score ✨. Click Posts Held or Posts in KPI to peek at the stars for each job!

How do we give the Perception Score? 😊
Asking lots of people how happy they are!
This score is like asking LOTS of people in many towns 🏘️: "How happy are you with the work?" 😊 We collect their happiness ratings for the things (called campaigns) each officer worked on. The longer an officer worked on something, the more it counts — just like earning more points for playing a game longer! 🎮

Which happiness scores does each officer get? 🤔

🏛️
SECRETARIAT
Department Boss
Runs a department from head office → gets happiness scores for all that department's work, from every town.
👑
HoD
Wing Boss
Head of a wing → gathers happiness scores for everything that wing did, everywhere.
⭐
CHIEF SECRETARY
The Biggest Boss
The top boss gets the happiness scores of ALL departments across the whole state!
🌾
DISTRICT • REVENUE
District Collector
Looks after a whole district → gets the scores of everything happening in their district.
🏢
DISTRICT • OTHER
District Officer
Gets scores for their own department's work, but only in their own district.
⏳ We give more weight to the months an officer actually held the job. Then we mix all their jobs together (longer jobs count more) to make one final happy score 🎉. Click Posts Held or Posts in Campaigns to see the scores for each job!

How do we give the e-Office Score? 🖥️
A friendly report card for finishing files!
Imagine every officer has a big basket of homework — these are called files 📄. This score is like a report card 🏆 that checks how well they finish their work. We look at 7 things, give everyone a fair score out of 100, and line them up like a friendly race! 🏁
✅
28% IMPORTANT
Finishing Work
Did they finish the files in their basket? More finished = higher marks!
🧹
17%
Clearing Old Piles
Did they clear the old waiting files? A tidy desk is a happy desk!
⚡
18%
Being Quick
How fast did they usually finish one file? Faster is better!
🐢
12%
No Slow Days
Even counting the tricky slow files, were they still speedy overall?
📚
10%
Handling a Lot
Some officers get LOADS of files. Handling more earns extra points.
📅
10%
Showing Up
How many days did they actually work? More days means more effort.
⚖️
5%
Being Steady
Were they even and reliable every time — not super fast one day and slow the next?
🎯 We compare everyone fairly (so one super-speedy officer doesn't spoil the game), then find who is closest to being the best all-rounder. That gives each officer a score out of 100! Scores update whenever you change a filter, because everyone is always judged against the group on screen. 👫
