⭐ Swarnandhra KPI — Scoring Methodology

1 · Purpose & design principles

The Swarna Andhra Pradesh programme tracks government performance through Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), scored 0–100, at two levels: by district and by department / HoD. This methodology attributes those KPI scores to the officer in charge of that district or department during the period.

PrincipleHow we achieve it
Right KPIs to the right officerThe post's Office Status picks the district vs department vs HoD KPI set.
Fair within a unitWhere a unit has several KPI rows, the post takes their average.
Longer postings count morePosts are combined by a tenure-days weighted average.
Already on a 0–100 scaleKPI scores are 0–100, so averaging keeps the result 0–100.

2 · The raw data

  • District KPI table — one or more KPI score rows per district.
  • Department / HoD KPI table — KPI score rows tagged with both a department and an HoD.

3 · Aggregating the KPI source into lookups

Before scoring officers, the source rows are aggregated into three averaged lookups (mean of all KPI rows for each key):

byDistrict[d] = mean( scores where district = d )
byDept[dep] = mean( scores where department = dep )
byHoD[h] = mean( scores where HoD = h )

So if a district has 5 KPI rows, byDistrict holds their simple average — one representative KPI number for that district. The same is done by department and by HoD.

4 · How posts map to KPIs

Each post's Office Status selects which averaged lookup applies:

Office StatusKPI attributed to the postSource
DistrictThe district's average KPI for the post's location.byDistrict
HoDThe average KPI of all rows under that HoD.byHoD
SecretariatThe average KPI of all rows for that Department.byDept
Post Score = mean of the applicable KPI rows = ( Σ scores ) ÷ ( count of scores )
A District post is judged on the place it administers; a Secretariat post on the department it runs from the head office; an HoD post on the wing it heads. A post whose unit has no KPI rows simply carries no score and is skipped (it does not count as zero).

5 · Across posts — the tenure-proportionate officer score

An officer's posts are combined into one number with a tenure-days weighted average, so longer service in a post carries proportionally more weight:

Officer Score = Σ ( PostScorep × tenureDaysp ) ÷ Σ ( tenureDaysp )

The sum runs only over posts that have a KPI score (posts in KPI). tenureDaysp is the officer's days in post p (from-date to to-date, or to today if still serving). This is the same proportionate-across-posts principle used by the Public Perception and GoI-Funds scores, so the three are directly comparable.

6 · A worked example

An officer with two posts

Post 1 — District Collector, Kurnool, held 450 days. Kurnool has KPI rows averaging 72.0 → Post Score 72.0.

Post 2 — Secretariat, Agriculture Dept, held 300 days. Agriculture KPI rows average 84.0 → Post Score 84.0.

Officer Score = (72.0 × 450 + 84.0 × 300) ÷ (450 + 300)
  = (32,400 + 25,200) ÷ 750 = 76.8 / 100

Both posts contribute, but the longer Kurnool tenure pulls the blended score toward its 72.

Averaging within a unit

If Agriculture has three KPI rows — 80, 86, 86 — the department's representative KPI is their mean:

byDept[Agriculture] = (80 + 86 + 86) ÷ 3 = 84.0

That single 84.0 is what the Secretariat post in the example above used.

7 · Glossary of statistical terms

KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
A 0–100 measure of government performance under the Swarna Andhra Pradesh framework, recorded by district and by department/HoD.
Aggregated lookup
A pre-computed average of all KPI rows for a key (a district, a department, or an HoD) — one representative number per unit.
Office Status
The nature of a post — District, HoD, or Secretariat — which selects the district / HoD / department KPI lookup.
Post Score
The average KPI of the unit a post administers.
Tenure days
The number of days an officer held a post — the weight used to combine posts.
Weighted arithmetic mean
An average where some values count more than others; here posts are weighted by tenure days.
Proportionate score
The principle that an officer's score reflects how long they served in each post, so brief postings cannot dominate long ones.
